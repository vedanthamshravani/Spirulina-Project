What is Spirulina?

Spirulina is a blue-green algae, and is believed to be one of the oldest life forms on Earth. Spirulina is considered a superfood — an all-in-one source of nutrients including protein levels comparable to eggs.

Spirulina is a good source of:
Thiamine (vitamin B1)
Riboflavin (vitamin B2)
Niacin (vitamin B3)
Copper
Iron

Spirulina also contains magnesium. This mineral supports normal daily functions like muscle use and your heartbeat. It’s also responsible for producing protein and creating energy — but most people don’t get enough in their diet.

Any harm by using Spirulina ? Spirulina is usually considered safe for everyone to consume taking in consideration of the doses. It is widely recognized as a nutrient-rich superfood and is often consumed as a dietary supplement or as an ingredient in various food products. Pregnant women and a person with any prior health conditions would be recommended to speak with their doctor before consumption .

Allergic to Spirulina? Symptoms of an allergic reaction to Spirulina may include itching, rash, hives, swelling, breathing difficulties, and anaphylaxis in severe cases. If you experience any of these symptoms after taking Spirulina powder, stop using it immediately, and seek medical attention if necessary.

HealthBenefits

Spirulina is a potent source of nutrients. It contains a powerful plant-based protein called phycocyanin which acts as an antioxidant, pain-relief, anti-inflammatory.

Research has found that the protein in spirulina can reduce the body’s absorption of cholesterol, lowering cholesterol levels. This helps keep your arteries clear, reducing strain on your heart that can lead to heart disease and stroke-causing blood clots. The anti-inflammatory effect caused by spirulina’s antioxidants may help people with allergies caused by pollen, animal hair, and dust. Spirulina also boosts the production of white blood cells and antibodies that fight viruses and bacteria in your body.
